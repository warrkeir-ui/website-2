A Welcome from Our Owner

Hello and welcome to Hakuna Matatah Preschool and Childcare Center! I'm Stefanie Boyd, and I'm so glad you're here. With over 20 years of experience in early childhood education, I've had the joy of watching countless children grow and thrive. My passion is to create a nurturing and loving environment where every child feels safe, cared for, and excited to learn. I believe in treating each child with the same love and kindness I would my own, and it's my promise to you that your little one will be in good hands.

I understand that choosing the right care for your child is one of the most important decisions you'll make. That's why I am dedicated to being accommodating to your family's needs and maintaining an open, trusting relationship with every parent. Thank you for considering Hakuna Matatah. I can't wait to meet you and your child!

Enrollment Information

Thank you for your interest in Hakuna Matatah Preschool and Childcare Center. We are dedicated to providing quality care for your child and ensuring all licensing requirements are met. Please read our contract and fee schedule below.

Fee Schedule and Policies

Tuition payments are due on Mondays at the start of each week. Billing will be done on Tuesday, and all late fees will be added if tuition has not been paid in full as directed. A late fee of $35.00 will be charged on Tuesday if tuition has not been received at that time. An additional $50.00 will be added if tuition has not been paid by Wednesday.

Hours of operation are from 7:00 am to 6:00 pm. Extended hours are available for those families in need. There will be a late charge of $5 per minute if children are not picked up at the contracted time. Please be on time to pick up your child(ren). Payments are accepted via cash, debit card, and checks. However, returned checks will be charged a $35.00 NSF fee, and we will not be able to accept payments via check again.

Simba Classroom (Infant)

The Simba classroom is designed for our youngest learners. We provide a safe, nurturing, and stimulating environment to support their rapid growth and development.

Curriculum Agenda

Our infant curriculum focuses on key developmental milestones, including:

  • Sensory Exploration: Activities that engage their senses of touch, sight, and hearing.
  • Gross Motor Skills: Tummy time, reaching for toys, and supported sitting to build strength and coordination.
  • Cognitive Development: Responding to vocalizations, peek-a-boo, and object permanence games.
  • Language Skills: Reading books, singing songs, and engaging in responsive conversations to encourage early communication.
  • Social-Emotional Connections: Building secure attachments through consistent, loving care and routines.

Daily Schedule

The daily schedule for our infant and toddler classrooms is designed to be flexible and responsive to the children's individual needs.

7:00 AM - 9:00 AM Arrival & Free Play: Greet children, prepare for the day.
9:00 AM - 9:30 AM Morning Snack: Healthy snacks are provided.
9:30 AM - 10:30 AM Activity Time: Arts & Crafts, reading, and sensory play.
10:30 AM - 11:15 AM Outdoor Play: If weather permits.
11:15 AM - 12:15 PM Quiet Time & Story Time: Winding down for lunch.
12:15 PM - 1:00 PM Lunch Time: Nutritious lunch is served.
1:00 PM - 1:30 PM Quiet Time & Story Time: Preparing for rest.
1:30 PM - 3:00 PM Nap Time / Quiet Rest: Individualized rest time.

4:15 PM - 5:15 PM Outdoor Play: Last call for outdoor fun.
5:15 PM - 6:00 PM Quiet Play & Dismissal: Books, puzzles, and awaiting pick-up.

Timon Classroom (Toddler)

In the Timon classroom, we focus on helping toddlers explore their world with a sense of wonder and independence. Our curriculum supports their growing curiosity and physical abilities.

Curriculum Agenda

Our toddler curriculum focuses on key developmental milestones, including:

  • Independent Exploration: Allowing children to choose their own activities and build confidence.
  • Social Skills: Learning to share, take turns, and interact with peers.
  • Language Development: Expanding vocabulary through stories, songs, and conversations.
  • Fine and Gross Motor Skills: Activities like stacking blocks, drawing, and running and jumping to build coordination.

Pumba Classroom (Preschool)

The Pumba classroom prepares children for kindergarten by focusing on school readiness skills. Our curriculum is designed to foster a love of learning and build a strong academic foundation.

Curriculum Agenda

Our preschool curriculum includes:

  • Literacy: Phonics, letter recognition, and early reading skills.
  • Numeracy: Counting, number recognition, and basic math concepts.
  • Science and Social Studies: Hands-on experiments and exploring the world around them.
  • Social-Emotional Development: Learning to cooperate, solve conflicts, and express feelings.
  • Creative Arts: Music, art, and dramatic play to encourage self-expression.

Daily Schedule

Time Activity
7:00 AM - 8:30 AM Arrival & Center Time: Free play and greeting friends.
8:30 AM - 9:00 AM Morning Circle: Calendar, weather, songs, and story time.
9:00 AM - 9:30 AM Morning Snack: Community snack time.
9:30 AM - 10:30 AM Academic Centers: Small group activities focused on literacy and math.
10:30 AM - 11:30 AM Outdoor Play: Large motor skill development.
11:30 AM - 12:00 PM Read Aloud & Transition: Preparing for lunch.
12:00 PM - 12:45 PM Lunch Time: Nutritious meal served.
12:45 PM - 1:00 PM Quiet Time: Wind down with quiet stories or soft music.
1:00 PM - 2:30 PM Nap Time / Quiet Rest: Rest time or quiet activities.
2:30 PM - 3:00 PM Afternoon Snack: Refueling for the afternoon.
3:00 PM - 4:00 PM Project & Art Time: Hands-on creative activities.
4:00 PM - 5:00 PM Outdoor Play / Free Play: Final play period.
5:00 PM - 6:00 PM Clean-up & Dismissal: Packing up and awaiting parent pick-up.

Zazu Classroom (Aftercare)

Our Zazu Aftercare program provides a safe, fun, and engaging environment for school-aged children after their school day. We offer a mix of structured activities and free play.

Program Agenda

The aftercare program is designed to be a relaxing and enriching extension of the school day. Activities include:

  • Homework Help: Dedicated time and support for completing school assignments.
  • Creative Play: Access to art supplies, board games, and building blocks.
  • Outdoor Recreation: Time for sports and games to release energy.
  • Group Activities: Storytelling, team-building games, and special projects.
  • Social Interaction: Encouraging positive relationships among peers.
